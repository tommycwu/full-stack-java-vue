Case Study

Sally Field is interviewing with Okta Ice to take on the role of Lead Okta Admin. She will be provided an Okta Admin account, which has unrestricted access to two Okta Preview orgs, to show her future management team that she can successfully support the onboarding and offboarding of interns who will be joining Okta Ice in the next quarter. The first Okta org will serve as a the Identity Provider (org1), while the second Okta org will serve as a Service Provider (org2). This Case Study provides a high-level overview of the tasks Sally will be asked to perform on the Hands-on Configuration Exam. The actual tasks are provided as Use Cases 1-4, which can be accessed through the other tabs on this page. The Okta Org credentials you will need for the exam are provided in the tab labeled “Org Info.” Specific details for configuration and testing are provided in the use cases. Sally must complete the exam within 120 minutes after which her access to the two Okta orgs will be revoked. Sally’s performance on the tasks in the exam will then be evaluated and scored. During the exam, you will play the role of Sally. You are charged with performing the tasks in the use cases that have been assigned to Sally. If you perform enough of the tasks correctly to achieve the minimum score required to pass the exam, Sally will be hired for the role, and you, in return, will be granted the Okta Certified Professional Certification.

Overview of Tasks Sally Needs to Perform

1. Sally will be given an account to log in to Okta as a Super Admin.

2. She will create an account for Sanjay Patel, who will assist her with managing help desk requests in org1 as an admin. Sanjay will be given an Okta admin role to perform help desk duties, such as password resets.

3. Mike Barnes will be Sally’s test Intern user. Sally will need to create and activate his account. After his account is created, Sally will define some key attributes for his account and Sanjay’s.

4. To ensure all Interns are assigned the correct apps upon hire, Interns need to be dynamically assigned to an Interns group in Okta. Sally will create an Interns group and define logic in Okta, in which the department attribute for Interns will be used to dynamically define membership.

5. All members of the Interns group in Okta will be granted access to the second or org2, which requires the setup of SAML federation in Okta and the service provider. At this time, only an IdP initiated flow is required for SSO, in which the user will access org2 org directly from their Okta Dashboard. Okta Ice does not have any plans to set up a custom domain in org2 to force an SP-initiated SSO flow. Sally has access to org2 through a service account and credentials to log in.

6. To provision assigned users into org2, Okta Lifecycle Management will be utilized in which a direct API integration will be set up between the two Okta orgs. Sally will utilize the provided org2 service account for the API integration. Setting up the API integration is critical to ensure new users are pushed into org2 when they are assigned to the application in Okta. This will also allow for key attributes in org2 to be updated based on changes in Okta. Lastly, users will be deactivated in org2 when the respective users in org1 are offboarded (deactivated).

7. From a security standpoint, MFA will be configured. Okta Verify and a Security Question will be leveraged as the MFA Factors.

8. MFA Enrollment will require Okta Verify to be set up as an optional factor, while a Security Question will be required. All Interns must enroll into MFA, the first time they sign into Okta, regardless of location. All Okta Admins, including Sanjay, will be excluded from being challenged for MFA.

9. Each time an Intern tries to log in to Okta from outside the Corporate Office, they must provide a 2nd factor of verification. They are not prompted for MFA when they click on org2 from the Okta Dashboard.

10. To simulate the end of the internship, Mike will be offboarded in org1. Before that occurs, Sally needs to ensure that org2 has the most up-to-date attribute data.

11. To test the offboarding flow/deactivation process, Mike will be manually deactivated in org1, which will trigger the Okta Lifecycle event in org2 to automatically deactivate his account.

NOTES:

The exam will be performed on your computer using any supported web browser. A virtual machine/server will not be needed. ONLY use the two Okta orgs you are assigned to take this exam.
